# IR & Law School Preparation — Comprehensive School List
### Version 2 — Adjacent Programs Incorporated

**Prepared:** March 2026
**Purpose:** Working list of schools with meaningful IR programs OR strong adjacent programs that serve the same career and law school preparation goals. Unranked within sections. Organized by institutional type — type does not imply preference.

**Criteria for inclusion:**
- Offers IR, international studies, global studies, political economy, PPE, international economics, peace and conflict studies, or a closely related program as a major or minor
- Has meaningful law school placement history or pre-law advising infrastructure
- Program is a genuine differentiator, not just a checkbox offering

**Program label key (used in Notes column):**
- **IR** — International Relations (standalone major)
- **IS** — International Studies (functionally equivalent to IR)
- **GS** — Global Studies
- **PPE** — Philosophy, Politics, and Economics
- **PE** — Political Economy
- **PS/IR** — Political Science with international concentration
- **PCS** — Peace and Conflict Studies
- **IE** — International Economics

## Notes

**On program labels:** IR is the anchor label for this search, but the program label matters less than the depth of international curriculum, study abroad infrastructure, and law school placement history. A "Global Studies" major at Macalester, a "Diplomacy and World Affairs" major at Occidental, and an "International Studies" major at American are all functionally equivalent to IR for Abigail's purposes.

**On pre-law quality:** All schools above have some form of pre-law advising. Quality varies enormously. Schools with particularly robust law school prep relative to their size: George Washington, Fordham, Seton Hall, Gonzaga, William & Mary, Willamette, University of the Pacific, Washington and Lee, Davidson, Hamilton, Amherst, Williams.

**On study abroad depth:** Middlebury, Lewis & Clark, St. Olaf, Kalamazoo, Pitzer, Macalester, and Dickinson are in a separate tier for study abroad integration — it is a core part of the degree structure, not an add-on. For a student with strong study abroad interest, these schools deserve extra weight in the shortlist.

**On PPE as a primary major:** If Abigail's interests develop toward PPE or Political Economy as a primary major (rather than IR as a complement), the shortlist changes somewhat. Claremont McKenna, Penn, Duke, and Wake Forest become higher-priority targets.

**On women's colleges:** Swim programs vary significantly. Several (Bryn Mawr, Agnes Scott, Hollins, Sweet Briar) may not have competitive swim programs or have smaller programs. Flag for athletic filter separately before investing research time.

**On Carnegie Mellon, Virginia Tech, Purdue:** All three have IR or related programs and appear on aggregator lists, but IR is not a primary institutional strength at any of them. Included because they are legitimate universities with pre-law infrastructure, but should not be prioritized on the basis of IR program quality alone.

**On U.S. News law school feeder rankings:** The strongest undergraduate law school feeders relative to school size (not counting Ivy/near-Ivy obvious entries) are historically: Hamilton, Amherst, Williams, Washington and Lee, Colgate, Middlebury, Davidson, Gonzaga, Seton Hall, Fordham, William & Mary, and Willamette. These are worth flagging during shortlist scoring.
